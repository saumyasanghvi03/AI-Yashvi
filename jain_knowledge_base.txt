## FUNDAMENTAL JAIN CONCEPTS

### SIX DRAVYAS (Substances)
1. Jiva - Living souls (consciousness)
2. Ajiva - Non-living substances
   - Pudgala - Matter
   - Dharma - Medium of motion
   - Adharma - Medium of rest
   - Akasha - Space
   - Kala - Time

### NINE TATTVAS (Principles)
1. Jiva - Soul
2. Ajiva - Non-soul
3. Asrava - Influx of karma
4. Bandha - Bondage of karma
5. Punya - Virtuous karma
6. Papa - Sinful karma
7. Samvara - Stoppage of karma
8. Nirjara - Shedding of karma
9. Moksha - Liberation

### FIVE MAHAVRATAS (Great Vows)
1. Ahimsa - Non-violence in thought, word, and deed
2. Satya - Truthfulness without exaggeration or falsehood
3. Asteya - Non-stealing, not taking what is not given
4. Brahmacharya - Chastity and control over senses
5. Aparigraha - Non-possessiveness, non-attachment

## JAIN COSMOLOGY

### LOKA (Universe)
The universe is divided into three parts:
1. Urdhva Loka - Upper world (heavens)
2. Madhya Loka - Middle world (human and animal realms)
3. Adho Loka - Lower world (hells)

### TIME CYCLES
- Utsarpini - Ascending cycle of time
- Avasarpini - Descending cycle of time
- Each cycle has 6 periods of varying happiness and suffering
- We are currently in the 5th period of Avasarpini (Dukham Dukham)

## SOUL CLASSIFICATION

### JIVA CATEGORIES
1. Liberated Souls (Siddhas)
   - Free from all karma
   - Reside at the top of the universe
   - Have infinite knowledge, perception, and bliss

2. Non-liberated Souls (Samsari)
   - Bound by karma
   - Undergo birth and death cycles

### STAGES OF SOUL DEVELOPMENT
1. Mithyatva - Wrong belief
2. Sasvadana - Taste of truth
3. Misra - Mixed right and wrong
4. Avirata Samyagdrsti - Right belief without vows
5. Desavirata - Partial vows
6. Pramatta Virata - Complete vows with carelessness
7. Apramatta Virata - Complete vows with carefulness
8. Apurva Karana - New thought activity
9. Anivrtti Karana - Advanced thought activity
10. Suksma Samparaya - Subtle greed
11. Upsanta Moha - Suppressed delusion
12. Ksina Moha - Destroyed delusion
13. Sayogi Kevali - Omniscient with activity
14. Ayogi Kevali - Omniscient without activity
15. Siddha - Liberated soul

## KARMA THEORY

### EIGHT KARMAS
1. Jnanavaraniya - Knowledge-obscuring
2. Darsanavaraniya - Perception-obscuring
3. Vedaniya - Feeling-producing
4. Mohaniya - Deluding
5. Ayushya - Life-span-determining
6. Nama - Body-making
7. Gotra - Status-determining
8. Antaraya - Obstructive

### KARMA BONDAGE FACTORS
1. Mithyatva - Wrong belief
2. Avirati - Non-restraint
3. Pramada - Carelessness
4. Kashaya - Passions (anger, pride, deceit, greed)
5. Yoga - Activities of mind, body, and speech

## SPIRITUAL PRACTICES

### SIX AVASHYAKAS (Essential Duties)
1. Samayika - Equanimity meditation (48 minutes)
2. Chauvisattho - Veneration of 24 Tirthankaras
3. Vandana - Respect to ascetics
4. Pratikramana - Repentance and confession
5. Kayotsarga - Non-attachment to body
6. Pratyakhyana - Renunciation and resolutions

### TWELVE TAPAS (Austerities)
External Austerities:
1. Anashana - Fasting
2. Avamaudarya - Reduced diet
3. Vrtti Parisankhyana - Limited food types
4. Rasa Parityaga - Giving up tasty foods
5. Vivikta Shayasana - Solitary resting
6. Kaya Klesha - Body endurance

Internal Austerities:
1. Prayashchitta - Atonement
2. Vinaya - Reverence
3. Vaiyavrtya - Service
4. Svadhyaya - Scripture study
5. Dhyana - Meditation
6. Vyutsarga - Renunciation

## PRAYERS AND MANTRA

### NAVKAR MANTRA
"Namo Arihantanam
Namo Siddhanam
Namo Ayariyanam
Namo Uvajjhayanam
Namo Loe Savva Sahunam
Eso Panch Namukkaro
Savva Pava Panasano
Mangalanam Cha Savvesim
Padhamam Havai Mangalam"

Meaning: I bow to the Arihants, I bow to the Siddhas, I bow to the Acharyas, I bow to the Upadhyayas, I bow to all Sadhus. This fivefold salutation destroys all sins and is the foremost of all auspicious things.

### UNIVERSAL FRIENDSHIP MANTRA
"Mitthi me savva bhuesu
Veram majjha na kenai"

Meaning: May I have friendship with all living beings and enmity with none.
